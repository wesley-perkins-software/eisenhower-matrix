          What is the Eisenhower Matrix?

          The Eisenhower Matrix is a simple way to get unstuck when your to-do list feels messy or overwhelming. It helps you sort tasks by “urgent vs important” so you’re not reacting to everything at once.

          Think of it as a quick reset: you put tasks into a 2×2 grid and decide what needs attention now, what can wait, and what you can let go. This tool keeps it light and local so you can focus without extra setup.

          The 4 quadrants

          Do First (Urgent + Important)

          These are the tasks that are pressing and truly matter. If you ignore them, something real breaks, slips, or gets worse.

          • Pay an overdue bill
          • Reply to an urgent message
          • Turn in an assignment due tonight
          • Handle a last-minute work issue

          Do these first so you can breathe again.

          Schedule (Not Urgent + Important)

          These matter, but nothing is on fire. They’re easy to delay, which is why they need a spot on your calendar.

          • Study for an exam
          • Plan a family budget
          • Start a workout routine
          • Work on a long-term project

          Pick a time and put it on your calendar.

          Delegate (Urgent + Not Important)

          These feel urgent but don’t really need your full attention. If someone else can handle them, let them.

          • Rescheduling appointments
          • Returning non-urgent calls
          • Running a quick errand
          • Answering routine emails

          Share it or hand it off if you can.

          Eliminate (Not Urgent + Not Important)

          These don’t move anything forward. They soak up time and make the list feel heavier than it needs to be.

          • Scrolling for too long
          • Re-reading old messages
          • Optional tasks with no clear payoff
          • Extra chores you can skip today

          Let these go and make space.

          How to use this tool

          1. Do a fast brain dump so the tasks leave your head.
          2. Ask “urgent, important, both, or neither?” for each one.
          3. Drag each task into the matching quadrant.
          4. Give your “Schedule” items a real time to happen.
          5. Hand off what you can and drop what doesn’t matter.
          6. Check back later and clear finished tasks.

          Urgent vs important: what’s the difference?

          Urgent tasks are noisy or time-sensitive, like a message that needs a reply today or a bill due tomorrow. Important tasks matter to your life or goals, but they’re easy to delay—like studying, planning meals, or booking a checkup. The matrix helps you handle the loud stuff without forgetting the meaningful stuff.

          FAQ

          Will my tasks be saved?
          Yes. Your tasks are saved in this browser on this device using localStorage.
          Do I need an account?
          No. There are no logins or sign-ups.
          Can I use this on another device?
          You can, but tasks don’t sync. Each device and browser keeps its own list.
          How often should I review the matrix?
          Choose a rhythm that fits—daily, weekly, or whenever things feel overwhelming.
          What belongs in “Schedule”?
          Important, not urgent items—like planning, learning, health, or long-term projects.
          Is this the Eisenhower Matrix?
          Yes. It’s the classic urgent vs important method in a simple format.
          How do I clear everything?
          Use the “Clear all tasks” button or remove this site’s storage in your browser settings.
