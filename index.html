          What is the Eisenhower Matrix?

          The Eisenhower Matrix is a simple way to prioritize tasks by separating what is urgent from what is important. Instead of reacting to every notification, you decide where each task belongs in a 2×2 grid. That keeps your focus on high-impact work while still handling the true emergencies.

          This tool lets you capture tasks quickly, then move them into the right quadrant. Everything stays on your device, so you can make decisions fast without creating accounts or syncing data.

          The 4 quadrants

          Do First (Urgent + Important)

          These are the tasks with immediate deadlines or real consequences. If you delay them, you create more risk or cost, so they should be done now.

          • Fix a production outage
          • Submit a deadline-sensitive proposal
          • Respond to a critical customer issue
          • Prepare for today’s meeting

          Recommended action: Do now.

          Schedule (Not Urgent + Important)

          This quadrant is about long-term value: planning, learning, and relationship building. These tasks move the needle, but they are easy to postpone unless you schedule them.

          • Write a project roadmap
          • Train for a certification
          • Plan next quarter goals
          • Schedule a 1:1 with a mentor

          Recommended action: Schedule it.

          Delegate (Urgent + Not Important)

          These tasks feel pressing but don’t require your unique skills. If someone else can do them, move them off your plate so you can stay focused.

          • Routine status updates
          • Meeting logistics
          • Basic data cleanup
          • Scheduling customer demos

          Recommended action: Delegate.

          Eliminate (Not Urgent + Not Important)

          These tasks add little value and can distract you from meaningful work. If they do not serve a real goal, remove them or say no.

          • Low-impact meetings
          • Re-checking dashboards with no decisions
          • Optional tasks with no outcome
          • Scrolling through outdated inbox threads

          Recommended action: Eliminate.

          How to use this tool

          1. Do a quick brain dump and add every task.
          2. Decide if each task is urgent, important, both, or neither.
          3. Drag tasks into the right quadrant.
          4. Schedule time for the “Schedule” items.
          5. Delegate what you can and remove what you should not do.
          6. Review daily or weekly and clear completed items.

          Urgent vs important: what’s the difference?

          Urgent tasks demand immediate attention and often have a deadline. Important tasks contribute to long-term goals and meaningful outcomes. An urgent task might be “respond to a bug report,” while an important task might be “improve reliability so bugs happen less often.” The matrix helps you handle what is urgent without neglecting what is important.
