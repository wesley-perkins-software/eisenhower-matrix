          What is the Eisenhower Matrix?

          The Eisenhower Matrix is a simple priority matrix that helps with task prioritization by separating what is urgent vs important. It is a practical time management approach that turns a long list into four clear categories.

          This productivity tool keeps the method lightweight: add tasks, place them into a quadrant, and focus on what matters first. There is no sign up, and your list stays on this device.

          The 4 quadrants

          Do First (Urgent + Important)

          These tasks need attention soon and have real impact. They are time-sensitive and meaningful, so they should be handled first.

          • Reply to a time-sensitive request
          • Pay a bill due today
          • Finish an assignment due soon
          • Resolve a work issue with a deadline

          Focus here to reduce immediate pressure.

          Schedule (Not Urgent + Important)

          These tasks matter, but they are not urgent yet. Planning them protects your long-term goals.

          • Plan the next week
          • Work on a long-term project
          • Build a learning habit
          • Book a routine appointment

          Put these on your calendar so they happen.

          Delegate (Urgent + Not Important)

          These tasks feel urgent but do not require your specific attention. If possible, share or delegate them.

          • Schedule a meeting you do not need to lead
          • Handle a routine request
          • Respond to a non-critical message
          • Follow up on a standard form or document

          Ask for help or set a simple handoff.

          Eliminate (Not Urgent + Not Important)

          These tasks add little value and can distract from what matters. Consider removing them or saving them for later if they are optional.

          • Optional tasks with no clear benefit
          • Low-value notifications to check
          • Busywork that does not move goals forward
          • Tasks you added without a clear reason

          Let these go and make space.

          How to use this tool

          1. Add tasks quickly so they are out of your head and in one place.
          2. Decide if each task is urgent, important, both, or neither.
          3. Drag tasks into the matching quadrant.
          4. Give “Schedule” items a specific date or time.
          5. Delegate or simplify tasks that are urgent but not important.
          6. Review the list regularly and clear completed tasks.

          Urgent vs important: what’s the difference?

          Urgent tasks are time-sensitive and need attention soon. Important tasks support your goals, responsibilities, or wellbeing. The Eisenhower Matrix helps you act quickly where needed while protecting time for what matters.

          FAQ

          Will my tasks be saved?
          Yes. Your tasks are saved in this browser on this device using localStorage.
          Do I need an account?
          No. There is no sign up, login, or account setup.
          Can I use this on another device?
          You can, but tasks do not sync. Each device and browser keeps its own list.
          Does this tool work offline?
          It may continue to work after the page has loaded once, but offline use is not guaranteed.
          Is my data private?
          Your tasks stay in your browser. There is no backend or account tied to your data.
          What happens if I clear my browser data?
          If you clear site data or localStorage, your saved tasks will be removed.
          What is the difference between urgent and important?
          Urgent means time-sensitive. Important means it supports a goal or responsibility even if it can wait.
          What is a priority matrix?
          A priority matrix is a simple framework for sorting tasks by value and urgency. The Eisenhower Matrix is a well-known example.
          How do I decide where a task belongs?
          Ask two questions: does it need attention soon, and does it meaningfully support your goals? Place it where the answers match.
          What should I do with tasks that feel urgent but are not important?
          Move them to “Delegate” if someone else can handle them, or simplify the task so it takes less of your time.
          Can I print or export my matrix?
          This tool does not include print or export features. You can copy tasks manually if needed.
          What happens if I use a different browser?
          Each browser stores its own localStorage, so your tasks will not appear elsewhere.
          How often should I update my matrix?
          Update it whenever priorities shift. Many people review daily or weekly to keep it accurate.
          What belongs in “Schedule”?
          Important tasks that are not urgent yet, such as planning, learning, or long-term projects.
          Is this the Eisenhower Matrix?
          Yes. It is the classic urgent vs important method presented in a simple layout.
          How do I clear everything?
          Use the “Clear all tasks” button or remove this site’s storage in your browser settings.
